Some of my work

9th Grade - Short Story

In my 9th grade English class, I was tasked with creating a 3-5 page short story. I decided to make my own spin-off story based on the world of the game Cyberpunk 2077. I recall investing a considerable amount of time and effort in this assignment, far more than any other assignment I undertook freshman year. I was commended for my use of descriptive language, and according to my English teacher at the time, left her wanting more as a reader.

10th Grade - American Heroes Tournament

My 10th Grade U.S. History final was a multi-assignment project that pitted each student in the class and their chosen "American Hero" against each other. While everyone else was choosing individuals like George Washington and Ben Franklin, I made the focus of my project Keith Gill, the man who led a successful short squeeze of GameStop stock on the internet. I wrote a biography, debated my classmates, designed and 3D printed a physical element (diamond), and made a video presentation that was deemed superior to the work of my peers via majority vote, winning Keith Gill and me the American Heroes tournament.

11th Grade - Hypothesis Testing

In my Math 110 class at UHMC, I was tasked with making a hypothesis given a data set and mathematically proving or falsifying my null hypothesis. For this project, I took a data set of graduation rates across different ethnicities to test the stereotype that Asians perform better in school than other races. Applying what I learned about hypothesis testing from that class, I calculated a p-value and test statistic, then graphed my results on a t-distribution to find that I should reject my null hypothesis.
